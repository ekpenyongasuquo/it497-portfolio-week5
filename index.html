Ethical Dilemma Analysis – Alternative Approach

The ethical dilemma involved balancing the need to track participant progress with the responsibility to protect privacy and dignity. Excessive personal data posed risks to trust and participant agency.

The ethical principles guiding my decision included honesty, responsibility, respect for privacy, transparency, and stewardship of information. Rather than collecting detailed personal data, I implemented an alternative approach using anonymized progress metrics and voluntary feedback.

This alternative resulted in better outcomes: participants felt respected and secure, trust increased, and the program retained sufficient data to improve instruction without compromising ethical standards.

IT Project: Refinement and Ethical Integration – Project Refinement Record

Initially, the project functioned as general technical support. Participant anxiety, limited hardware capabilities, and inconsistent learning outcomes revealed the need for refinement.

I transitioned the project into a structured instructional design model. Lessons were broken into small, achievable goals to reduce anxiety and increase confidence. Accessibility settings were configured for low vision and limited dexterity, and older hardware was optimized for performance.

Ethical considerations were integrated by treating participant data as a form of stewardship. Progress tracking was limited to essential anonymized metrics using Excel and Power BI. These refinements increased participant engagement, confidence, and learning consistency while maintaining ethical responsibility.
